About me

I am a postdoctoral research scientist with David Blei at Columbia University. I completed my PhD at University Carlos III in Madrid, under the supervision of Fernando Perez-Cruz. My research is focused on approximate inference, Bayesian nonparametric models and their applications. I am also affiliated to University of Sheffield and Gregorio Maranon Health Research Institute.

In 2013, I was a visiting student research collaborator at Princeton University, under the supervision of David M. Blei. In summer 2014, I did a research stay in University of Sheffield, being the supervisor Neil D. Lawrence.

See my CV for further information about me.

Research interests

I am interested in Bayesian nonparametric models and their applications, as well as the related inference algorithms (Markov chain Monte Carlo methods, variational inference, expectation propagation, etc.).
